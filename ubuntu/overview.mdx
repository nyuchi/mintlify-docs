The Nyuchi Design System is built on **Ubuntu** — the southern African philosophy
captured by the phrase *"I am because we are."* A person is a person through other
people. Identity, dignity, and meaning are relational, not individual.

Ubuntu is not decoration on top of the design system — it is the doctrine
underneath it. Every token, every component, and every product decision is made
with the community in mind. The system is open source and designed for anyone to
use, extend, and contribute to.

Ubuntu is expressed in the design system through two structured frameworks, both
maintained as canonical data in the platform database:

## Pillars and principles

The **Pillars** describe the *spheres* in which a person exists — the concentric
webs of belonging that define who someone is. The **Principles** describe the
*values* a person practises within those spheres. Together they translate Ubuntu
from philosophy into concrete platform behaviour.

Both frameworks are served live from the API:

```bash
curl https://design.nyuchi.com/api/v1/ubuntu/pillars
curl https://design.nyuchi.com/api/v1/ubuntu/principles
```

They are also exposed to AI assistants through the MCP server as the
`get_ubuntu_pillars` and `get_ubuntu_principles` tools, and as the
`mukoko://ubuntu` resource.
