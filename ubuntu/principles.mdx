---
title: "The Five Principles"
description: "The five practised values of Ubuntu — survival, solidarity, compassion, respect, and dignity — and how each is expressed in the platform."
---

Where the [Five Pillars](/ubuntu/pillars) describe the *spheres* a person exists
within, the Five Principles describe the **values a person practises** within
those spheres. They are drawn from Mbigi's Collective Fingers Theory and related
Ubuntu scholarship.

Each principle has a direct, concrete expression in the design system and
platform. The principles are maintained as canonical rows in the
`ubuntu_principles` table and served at `GET /api/v1/ubuntu/principles`.

## 1. Survival — *Kurarama*

Collective endurance. No one survives alone; basic needs are met through one
another. Reciprocal care sustains the community before any individual can
flourish.

- **Expression:** Offline-first architecture, shared-device support, a
  budget-hardware performance budget, an open data commons — it works in a
  village with 2G.

## 2. Solidarity — *Kubatana*

A firm and persevering commitment to the common good. Not vague compassion but
active allegiance to the welfare of the whole — *kubatana* means to hold
together.

- **Expression:** Circles, Nhimbe gatherings, Ubuntu contribution types
  (moderation, translation, curation, mentorship), collective ownership of the
  open data commons.

## 3. Compassion — *Tsitsi*

Active care, sympathy, and concern shown through helping, sharing, and welcoming.
*Tsitsi* extends beyond kin to all — a stranger in need is not a stranger to
compassion.

- **Expression:** Hospitality to newcomers, help-first interaction patterns,
  welcome flows, accessible defaults that do not punish people for the device
  they own.

## 4. Respect — *Ruremekedzo*

Unprejudiced consideration of another's privileges, beliefs, and norms. It
encompasses elder-respect (*ukuhlonipha*) but applies to every person, every age,
every belief.

- **Expression:** Account switching for shared devices, multi-language parity
  with no second-class language, no age-gate assumptions, elder-respect in
  greeting patterns and tone.

## 5. Dignity — *Chiremerera*

Inherent worth held by virtue of existence — irrespective of status, ethnicity,
age, or background. Humanity is sacred. Dignity is not earned and cannot be
revoked.

- **Expression:** No dark patterns, data sovereignty, verification without
  stigma, memorial states for ancestral accounts, never selling user attention.

---

*Source: Mbigi (1997), Collective Fingers Theory; with Du Toit, Poovan &
Engelbretch (2006) and Ramose (2002).*
